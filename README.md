# Degree vs. Skill: Are FJWU Students Truly Job-Ready?
### A Data Analytics Capstone Project — Department of Computer Science, FJWU Batch 2025

> *"High academic scores alone are not sufficient indicators of professional readiness."*

---

## Overview

This capstone project investigates whether Computer Science students at **Fatima Jinnah Women University (FJWU), Rawalpindi** are genuinely job-ready beyond their academic performance. Using survey data from **108 students**, the research analyzes the relationship between CGPA, practical skills, industry exposure, certifications, and employment confidence.

The project combines **data engineering**, **statistical analysis**, **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**, and an **interactive analytics dashboard** to produce measurable insights into the skills gap between academia and industry expectations.

## Key Findings

- **CGPA is not a reliable predictor of job readiness.** Students with a 3.5–4.0 CGPA were actually *less* likely to self-report as job-ready compared to students in the 2.5–3.0 range.
- **68% of students had no internship experience**, yet students with internships reported significantly higher confidence and practical competence.
- **68.2% of students believe skills matter more than GPA** for getting a job; only 0.9% prioritized GPA.
- The biggest self-reported interview weaknesses were: lack of experience (54%), low confidence (36%), and poor communication skills (28%).
- Critical skill gaps exist in **DevOps, cloud computing, agile methodologies, and project management** — all of which are increasingly demanded by industry.

## Methodology

The project followed a structured 7-step data analytics pipeline:

1. **Survey Design** — Google Form capturing CGPA, skills, certifications, internship status, and career concerns
2. **Data Collection** — 108 responses from FJWU CS students
3. **Data Cleaning** — Missing value handling, duplicate removal, text normalization
4. **Exploratory Data Analysis** — Distribution analysis, trend detection, readiness pattern identification
5. **Statistical Analysis** — Correlation analysis, internship impact evaluation, comparative skill assessment
6. **NLP Analysis** — Sentiment analysis, keyword extraction, and behavioral analysis on open-ended responses
7. **Dashboard Development** — Interactive HTML dashboard with Chart.js visualizations
